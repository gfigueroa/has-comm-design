Week 11 (updated on 11/6)

It’s the first week of November!

This week, we continued working on our LearningCSS website. Using “position: fixed”, we can make elements stay where they are when the page is being scrolled up and down. In addition, “display: inline” and margins left and right allow programmers to make lists horizontal.

We were also told to recreate the HAS Computer Science website with HTML and CSS. While troublesome, it was a great way to practice on margins, paddings, and borders. I even figured out how to use <textarea>. The function of placeholders seemed really cool.

HAHAHA They sound humorous, don’t they?
Guess what, we were messing with these properties this week XD

Again, we were working on the LearningCSS website. We learnt about the different CSS positioning like static, relative, and absolute. There is also a special case, where the parent is relative and the child is absolute. We were also introduced to a new property, called "float". It is usually used together with "position: relative". Floating to the right means that a div will stick to the right at all times, and otherwise.

Our homework for the weekend was to recreate the semantic table using CSS properties. To me, it was a piece of cake. I completed it in an hour.
