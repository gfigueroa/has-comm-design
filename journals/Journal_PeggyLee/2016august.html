Week 1 (updated on 8/28)

Even though our classes this week were very fundamental, they were essential to our success in being a good web designer. Following Mr. Fig’s instructions, we differentiated the characteristics between a good and bad website and created a rubric for our ideal site.

I disagreed with none of this week’s argument. My group mates and I all had the same perspective on how a good website should look in terms of context, layout, and organization.

However, after doing lots of online research, I totally agreed with the idea that websites should be organized and clearly labeled. Grouping text and media under headings saves the audience a lot of time.

I believe the central concept communicated this week is to have your audience in mind when creating a website. An engineer is more likely to need a site full of logic and equations than a pianist does. Similarly, a child may value images over text.

If I could repeat this week, I would look up slogans and pictures that attract people of different ages, careers, or ethnicity. Like stated previously, what people view as a "good website" depends on their needs and background.
