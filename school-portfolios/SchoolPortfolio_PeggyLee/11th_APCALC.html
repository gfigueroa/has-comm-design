I. Course Summary

AP Calculus AB is an Advanced Placement calculus course taken by high school students. The course is traditionally taken after precalculus and is the first calculus course offered at most schools except for the regular calculus class. The Pre-Advanced Placement pathway for math will help prepare students for further Advanced Placement classes and exams.


This course built strongly on the algebra concepts we had learned and also worked with much of the trigonometry we had learned in the past few years. One of the main focus of this class was to be able to reason and solve problems with different strategies rather than memorizing formulae. We were expected to master in the following areas: limits at points, limits as functions approach positive or negative infinity, continuity of functions, taking derivatives, the various applications of derivatives, the various methods of taking definite integrals, and using differntial equations to model real world occurrences mathematically. The following is a more detailed syllabus.


Chapter 1  Prerequisites for calculus

Chapter 2  Limits and continuity

Chapter 3  Derivatives

Chapter 4  More derivatives

Chapter 6  The definite integral

Chapter 7  Differential equations and mathematical modeling

Chapter 8  Applications of definite integrals
