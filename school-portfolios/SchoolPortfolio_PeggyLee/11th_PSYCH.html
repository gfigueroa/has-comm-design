I. Course Summary

This course was an engaging introduction to the essential topics in psychology. Throughout this study of human behavior and the mind, we gained insight into the history of the field of psychology, as well as explored current theories and issues in areas such as research psychology, clinical psychology, and social psychology. Each unit of study includes a presentation of information on prominent theorists, historical information and examples of research studies that pertain to the unit and individual assignments within that unit.


Unit 1: Introduction to Psychology

We learnt about research methods and pioneering psychologists.

Unit 2: Body and Mind

We discovered how the nervous system, brain, endocrine system, and genetics shape human thoughts and behaviors.

Unit 3: Learning and Cognition

We learnt about conditioning and memory.

Unit 4: Developmental Psychology

We investigated the stages of development and the physical, social and cognitive changes that take place during life.

Unit 5: Personality

We studied the psychological theories of motivation and emotion, as well as how the different approaches in psychology describe the development of personality.

Unit 6: Health Psychology

We learnt about how psychological disorders and stress can produce mental and physical effects on the body that have various causes and symptoms. Students will discover the many therapies mental health professionals use to help people.
