I. Course Summary

In this course, we were introduced to a range of new topics such as the conservation of matter, stoichiometry, kinetic molecular theory, chemical equilibrium, redox reactions, acid-base chemistry, organic chemistry, biochemistry, and nuclear chemistry. The following is a summary of what each topic is about.


Unit 1: What is chemistry and matter

Introduction to Chemistry, chemical and physical properties, scientific method, lab safety and equipment

Unit 2: Atoms and the periodic table

Atomic structure and models, elements, compounds, mixtures, periodicity, electron configuration

Unit 3: Chemical bonds and compounds

Bonding, types of compounds, nomenclature, Lewis dot structures, electronegativity and ionization

Unit 4: Conservation of matter and stoichiometry

Avogadro's number, molar mass, molecular weight, scientific notation, stoichiometry, reactions, deceomposition, combustion, synthesis, single and double replacement, redox reactions, theoretical yield, stoichiometry calculations

Unit 5: Gases and their properties

Kinetic molecular theory, properties of gases, diffusion of gases, the gas laws, standard temperature and pressure values, problem solving using the ideal gas law, Dalton's law of partial pressures, and Graham's Law

Unit 6: Acids and bases

Acids, bases, salt solutions, pH, buffers, Arrhenius, Bronsted-Lowry and Lewis acid-base definitions, chemical eqilibrium
