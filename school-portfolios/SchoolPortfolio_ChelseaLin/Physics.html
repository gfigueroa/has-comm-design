        This course will give a basic overview of Physics and prepare them for more in-depth studies in high school or university. We will study Newtonian mechanics, properties of matter, waves, electricity, field forces, magnetism and atomic physics. Students will gain an understanding of related vocabulary and concepts as well as be asked to apply them to both practical and theoretical situations and problems. Students will be applying math skills learned in Algebra courses and Geometry. This course expands on concepts introduced in Physics Science and prepares them for further study of Physics in AP level courses B and C. Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be expected to:
•Apply critical thinking skills to word problems.
•Resolve complex problems into easier components.
•Devise multiple strategies to approaching problems.
•Predict behavior of matter under various conditions.
•Validate predictions with application of appropriate formulas.
•Design simple experiments in the wake of the science method.
•Combine different formulas in order to solve problems.
•Construct force diagrams and determine the acceleration of an object.
•Reorganize complex circuits into simple circuits to determine voltage and current.
•Solve for the mechanical energy of a system.
•Resolve mechanical energy into its different forms.
•Confirm the behavior of mechanical and electromagnetic waves.
Must have completed Algebra II
